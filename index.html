I was born in Tianjin, China in 1993 and has been living there before I went to college. The people of the Tianjin City have quite an reputation for their senses of humor and casual living attitudes among Chinese people. And as someone who grew up there, I totally endorse such statement.
In 2011, I got admitted into Hohai University and started my career in water engineering ever since. After completed my B.E. in Agricultural Water Conservancy Engineering in 2015, I went to UC Irvine to continue my study in Hydrology & Water Resources as a Master student. I spent two wonderful years in California and happily graguated in 2017. I was brifely working at the CRAES in Beijing for a short period of time post my Master's.
In 2019, I came back to the States to pursue my PhD under the supervision of Dr. Tiantian Yang at OU.
For the reasearch, I am currently focusing on Hydrologic Forecasts with precipitation datasets from Numerical Weather Preictions (NWP).
